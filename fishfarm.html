Omorio Village

Fish Farming System, 2014 - Present

The families who have a stake in the first fish pond. They will take responsibility for its upkeep and security, as the primary beneficiaries of its production.

The Project

A large portion of Eastern Uganda is characterized by impoverished, rural farmland. Due to the impoverished nature of this region, minimal technical infrastructure is in place and consequently many communities are still lacking basic essentials. The main source of income for families living in this region is sustained through minimal surpluses from substance farming. As a result, there is little job specialization within this region. One Ugandan NGO, Serere Local Fish Farming Initiative (SELOCOFFI), set out to inspire members of its community to build their own fish ponds in order to supplement their income and create a community wide specialization. As a result, over thirty fish ponds have been built in the past six to eighteen months. Due to a lack of technical knowledge, SELOCOFFI has partnered with the Fordham Engineers Without Borders (EWB), in order to build a series of model fish ponds. These fish ponds will serve primarily as bases of knowledge for the community, yet they will be functioning sources of income for the community members as well.
